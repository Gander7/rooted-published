## Overview
A tag game where everyone lies face-down in pairs while a runner avoids IT by lying down next to a pair, forcing the person on the opposite end to become the new runner -- like Newton's Cradle.

## Setup
1. Have everyone find a partner and lie face-down on the ground side by side, scattered around the playing area.
2. Leave enough space between pairs so runners can move freely.
3. Choose one person to be IT and one person to be the runner. These two start standing.

## How to Play
1. IT chases the runner around the playing area, weaving between the pairs lying on the ground.
2. At any point, the runner can lie down next to any pair, creating a row of three.
3. When this happens, the person on the opposite end of the row (the one the runner did NOT lie next to) must immediately get up and become the new runner.
4. The new runner must now avoid being tagged by IT.
5. If IT tags the runner, the runner becomes the new IT, and the old IT becomes the runner (roles swap).
6. Play continues as long as desired.

## How to Win
There is no formal winner. The game is continuous. The goal is simply to avoid being IT.

## Variations
- Allow the pairs to lie on their backs so they can see the action better.
- Add a second IT for larger groups to increase intensity.
- Require the new runner to complete a full lap around the playing area before they can lie down next to a pair.

## Leader Notes
- Remind players that the person on the far end of the trio must get up immediately. Hesitation slows the game.
- Watch for collisions since runners are weaving between people lying on the ground.
- This game works best with at least 10 people (4 pairs + IT + runner). More people means more options and more fun.
